Types of Aquaculture

Mariculture

Mariculture, a subset of aquaculture, involves the cultivation of marine organisms in the ocean, enclosed sections of the ocean, or in tanks and ponds filled with seawater. This practice is critical for increasing seafood production, ensuring food security, and supporting economic development, especially in coastal regions.

Freshwater Aquaculture

Freshwater aquaculture involves the breeding, rearing, and harvesting of aquatic organisms in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, rivers, and tanks. It is a critical component of global food systems, contributing significantly to food security, nutrition, and livelihoods.

Integrated Multi-Trophic Aquaculture (IMTA)

Integrated Multi-Trophic Aquaculture (IMTA) is an innovative approach to aquaculture that combines the farming of multiple species from different trophic levels within the same system. The primary goal of IMTA is to create a balanced and sustainable ecosystem where the waste produced by one species serves as a resource for another. This method can enhance overall productivity, reduce environmental impacts, and improve the economic viability of aquaculture operations.
